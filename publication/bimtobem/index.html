Building energy modelling based on building information modelling: the remaining problems and a more robust method

Mingyue Guo
January, 0001
routine

Abstract

Obtaining information from BIM (building information modelling) can liberate engineers from the most time-consuming and labour-intensive work of building BEM (building energy modelling). Thus, it becomes a popular topic in both the research and the industrial society in recent years. However, there are no tools that can smoothly accomplish the transformation of a complex BIM to a reliable BEM. The biggest obstacle of BIM to BEM is the deviation of geometrical information, which can be summarized in two main aspects. One is the inaccurate information caused by the original BIM, another is the information deviation among BIM, intermedia files and BEM. A more robust method based on gbXML that can check, simplify and correct some complex geometric information is proposed in this paper.
